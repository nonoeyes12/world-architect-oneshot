# Output Contract — Sections & Required Fields

The framework produces these sections **in order**. Field lists are the coverage checklist for
each; the canonical, fully-tagged version is in [`../prompt/world-architect-oneshot.xml`](../prompt/world-architect-oneshot.xml).

1. **Campaign Title** — main title; subtitle; three alternates; one-sentence hook; one-paragraph pitch; tone tags; genre tags; best-fit player types; runtime; complexity; content rating.
2. **Surfaced Assumptions** — every assumption (rules, session, tone, content, player behavior, GM prep, setting, mechanical, pacing) and how it shapes the design.
3. **Design Targets** — what players feel at 10 min / halfway / near the climax; what they debate, fear, want, and remember; the table talk it should provoke; what the GM should emphasize and avoid.
4. **Player-Facing Pitch** *(spoiler-free)* — premise; tone; character fit; content expectations; what choices matter; an invitation line. No hidden truth.
5. **GM-Only Campaign Summary** — what's really happening; who caused the crisis; who's lying; what's misunderstood; the do-nothing outcome; what players can change; what can't be solved cleanly.
6. **World Bible** — Origin & Myth; Recent History; Geography & Environment; Politics & Power; Economy & Labor; Religion, Ritual & Taboo; Social Texture; Law, Crime & Punishment; Technology/Magic/Supernatural; **≥5 playable Contradictions**; Sensory Identity (5 sights/sounds/smells/textures/tastes/weather/motifs/phrases).
7. **The Hidden Truth** — what everyone thinks vs. what's real; why hidden; who knows; who suspects; who benefits; who'd be destroyed; the evidence; why it matters now; how it reframes the premise. Surprising but fair, and foreshadowed.
8. **Central Tension** — a pressure triangle/web: what Forces A/B/C want; how the environment presses; why ordinary people are caught; why the players matter; when it explodes.
9. **Factions & Power Map** *(3–6)* — per faction: name, symbol, public identity, private agenda, core belief, leader, face NPC, resources, territory, methods, internal division, what they know/misunderstand, what they want/offer/threaten, what they do if ignored, how they help/complicate the finale, a rumor, a secret. Then: alliance/rivalry/dependency maps, betrayals, negotiation levers, what changes if one falls.
10. **Key Locations** *(5–8, a network)* — per location: name; world & adventure function; first impression; visual; soundscape; smell; texture; current situation; NPCs; faction presence; what can be learned/done; hidden feature; interactive objects; hazard/opportunity; encounter trigger; secret route; how it changes with the clock; boxed text (≤120 words); GM notes. Plus default/sneaky/social/dangerous/hidden/discovery-gated routes.
11. **NPC Roster** *(8–12)* — include a dishonest patron, an understandable antagonist, a bystander who knows too much, a faction rep, a warm/comic character, a compromised helper, a victim with agency, a wildcard. Per NPC: name; pronouns; role; age impression; first impression; public/private motive; fear; desire; wound; secret; the lie; the avoided truth; want from players; what they can give; what they refuse; what changes their mind; ties to ≥2 NPCs; voice; mannerism; memorable line; if threatened/helped/ignored/exposed; table note. Playable in 30 seconds.
12. **Secrets, Clues & Revelations** — 10 secrets; 3 clues each; ≥2 discovery methods each; which locations/NPCs hold them; likely false readings; what confirms; what changes. Never depend on one roll or one NPC.
13. **One-Shot Structure** — a time-block runtime plan; the hook; the inciting incident; per-act goals/pressure/scenes/discoveries/complications/transitions and stall/rush/off-route handles; the point of no return.
14. **Opening Scene** — boxed text; sensory immersion; first NPC; a visible problem; a hidden clue; a reason to act now; a choice; three first actions with GM guidance; how to open out. Active within 5 minutes.
15. **Encounter Suite** *(6–10, mixed)* — per encounter: name; type; location; purpose; setup; stakes; what's happening on arrival; sensory description; opposition; player options; likely approaches; difficulty; twist; clues; rewards; costs; failure/partial/full states; escalation; de-escalation; improv notes; shorten; expand; mechanics.
16. **Exploration & Discovery Layer** — discovery by wandering/asking/studying/rule-breaking/helping/logistics/rituals/absence; plus 12 ambient discoveries, 8 rumors, 6 omens, 6 customs, 6 signs the truth is leaking.
17. **Social & Political Layer** — norms; etiquette costs; who talks/who needs leverage/who lies; bribes that work; threats that backfire; reputation; faction responses; a social consequence table.
18. **Combat / Danger / Conflict Layer** — threats; what makes each interesting; environmental complications; non-lethal stakes; ways to avoid/redirect/cost violence; enemy wants; morale/retreat/reinforcement triggers; collateral; stat suggestions or threat tiers.
19. **Mystery / Investigation Layer** — core & secondary questions; false/true leads; useful red herrings; the real timeline vs. the believed timeline; evidence & pressure maps; three-clue coverage; wrong-accusation, early-solve, and never-solve branches.
20. **Moral Dilemmas & Player Choices** *(≥5)* — per choice: situation; options A/B/C; why it's hard; who benefits/suffers; short- and long-term consequences; finale impact; how to keep it non-obvious. Cover truth-vs-stability, mercy-vs-justice, community-vs-freedom, loyalty-vs-principle, and power-sustained-by-harm.
21. **Escalation Clock** *(6–8 stages)* — per stage: trigger; visible sign; hidden change; faction move; NPC move; location change; new danger; new opportunity; how to slow/accelerate/redirect. Plus what advances/pauses/resets it and what happens if it fills.
22. **Living-World Tools** — a d12 events table, a d20 sensory-details table, a d10 rumor table, a d10 improvised-NPC table, and a d10 complication table.
23. **Treasure, Rewards, Boons, or Consequences** — material/social/faction/forbidden-knowledge/item/emotional/costly/cursed/reputation/seed rewards; per notable reward: name, description, use, cost, who wants it, how it changes play.
24. **Finale Options** *(a situation, not a cutscene; combat optional)* — location; what's happening on arrival; each faction's and NPC's aim; the environment; the truth fully revealed; three ways to begin; five player strategies; how prior choices alter it; complications; wonder/dread/sacrifice beats; a final moral choice; success/partial/failure outcomes; negotiation/revelation/sabotage/escape/combat/tragic/corrupt victories.
25. **Endings & Epilogues** — best-with-cost; bittersweet; pyrrhic; truth-exposed; truth-buried; faction victory; antagonist vindicated; catastrophic failure; weird-unresolved. Each with immediate / one-week / one-year fallout, what people say, what players regret, and a future seed.
26. **GM Running Notes** — consistency; pacing; off-route handling; improvising clues; distinct NPCs; escalation without railroading; shorten to 2h / expand to 6h / spread to a 3-session arc; dials for horror, humor, intrigue, heroism, tragedy.
27. **Player Handout** *(one page, spoiler-free)* — setting snapshot; what everyone knows; the crisis; character-creation guidance; useful customs; rumors; tie-in questions; a tone & safety note.
28. **Optional Pre-Generated Character Hooks** *(6–8)* — per hook: archetype; why you're here; what you want; who you know; what you fear; a secret; a faction with leverage; a question you want answered; why you'd cooperate; a personal stake in the finale.
29. **Safety & Tone Calibration** — content warnings; lines & veils; tone/horror/violence/humor/romance dials; consent check-ins; fade-to-black guidance; content-swap options that preserve stakes.
30. **World Continuity Map** — the connectedness ledger (see [`continuity-checkpoints.md`](continuity-checkpoints.md)).
31. **Final Condensed Run Sheet** — premise & hidden truth in three sentences each; main NPCs; factions; locations; essential clues; clock summary; timeline; encounter list; finale triggers; ending menu; emergency improv rules. Runnable at the table without the full document.

*(An optional Quality Assurance Pass — World Depth / Playability / Entertainment / Anti-Genericity
/ Table Usability checklists plus revision notes — is included in the XML for those who want the
model to self-audit before finalizing.)*
